Though it's just a timer app, it's the best timer app.

- In most timer apps, it takes about 6 keypresses to make a new timer and start it. Here, it takes just 1 or 2.

- It's designed to be comfortably usable one-handed. Every part of the app fits into the thumb zone, even in larger phones.

- When a timer goes off, timer apps generally wont just ring once, they'll keep ringing until you pull your phone up and unlock it and interact with the app to acknowledge the alarm. Sometimes you want this, but when you don't need it, it's an inconvenience, so we make it optional, you can have your alarms just make a sound once and then stop.

- The timers are visually compact and color-coded, so you can easily find and reuse the timers you use often, instead of recreating them every time. (*though, as stated above, creating and launching a new timer is so easy that it's often cognitively easiest to just recreate. A 9 minute timer can be created and launched with a single keystroke.*)

As far as we're aware, no other timer app has *any* of these advances, let alone all of them.

It also has a special feature called "timercules". Timercules are like containers that other timers can be dragged into. There are a few different kinds. "Sequence" timercules can be used for timing multi-stage cooking processes, or complex exercise routines. "Cycle" timercules can repeat, which can be used to make, for instance, pomodoro timers. Parallel timercules are a bit more obscure, but if you experiment a lot with recipes, you'll find a use for them.

And of course, we do have stopwatches.

Aside from that, it's just beautiful. It's built using a special animation framework that mako developed to make sure that layout reflows are always visually clear.

It's available on both platforms, right now.
